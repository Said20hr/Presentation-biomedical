The challenge of the subject

Digital technologies have taken hold in the healthcare sector. They make it possible to acquire and store information. Patient data, epidemiological data and medical knowledge are recorded on digital media.
Moreover, these data are usually expressed in an unstructured form, which makes their automated processing, and the effective access to useful information increasingly difficult.

The challenge of the subject

fully automated approaches of indexing systems seem essential. Although many efforts have been deployed for this purpose.
The indexing of complete biomedical texts according to segments specific to these texts, such as their title and summary, remains a real challenge .
Moreover, with large amounts of data, the use of partial information only to annotate documents is promising , furthermore the classification of texts in the medical field is quite difficult because of two main problems:

  • Multiple Grammatical errors

    present in the text

  • Multiple Medical techniques

    present in the text

What is the Objective of the subject ?

Classification consists of assigning one or more categories to a digital document.

  • Supervised learning.

    Creating rules from a training dataset containing examples processed and validated by an expert to automatically classify a new document

  • Information research

    studies how to find information in a corpus of documents.

  • Data mining

    seeks to extract knowledge from data, to discover patterns and to classify this data into categories

  • Natural language processing

    which provides technical support for the use of the document.
